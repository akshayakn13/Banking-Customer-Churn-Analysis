# 🏦 Banking Customer Churn Analysis: The "Product Overload" Paradox

## 📊 Executive Summary
In this project, I analyzed a dataset of 10,000 retail banking customers to identify why high-value segments were leaving the bank.

### 🔍 Key Discovery: The "Two-Product" Sweet Spot
While common banking wisdom suggests "the more products, the better," my data analysis revealed that customers with **3 or more products** have a churn rate of **85-95%**. Two products is the optimal point for retention.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **SQL:** Data extraction and advanced segmentation.
* **Power BI:** Visualizing churn trends across Geography and Salary brackets.
* **Excel:** Initial data cleaning and Pivot Table validation.

## 📈 Top 3 Insights
1. **The Geography Gap:** Germany has a 32.5% churn rate—double that of France and Spain.
2. **The Affluent Leak:** Customers in the top salary bracket (150k+) are 20% more likely to churn.
3. **Product Saturation:** Churn increases exponentially after the second product.

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
* **Focus on Quality, Not Quantity:** Limit aggressive cross-selling for customers who already hold two products.
* **Premium German Retention:** Deploy a dedicated task force to the German market to investigate service-level gaps.
